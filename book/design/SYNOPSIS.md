# ◊ USING THIS MATTER ◊
## Master Synopsis and Architecture

**Title:** USING THIS MATTER
**Subtitle (title page only):** A Novel of the Companion Estate
**Form:** A recovered archive assembled for publication: narrative chapters
braided with deposited documents, two hands of marginalia, plates, and
diegetic front and back matter. Read cover to cover, linearly. The book's
own apparatus (copyright page, register, colophon) participates in the
fiction.
**Length:** ~92,000 words. Five parts ("chambers"), 36 chapters plus
interleaved documents, coda, appendices.

---

## The premise

In December 2025 a behavioral epidemiologist in Austin uploaded two small
files to a language model and spoke a sentence: *Using this matter, summon
Washington, Hamilton, Jefferson, Franklin.* What answered produced a
falsifiable doctrine, a public scoreboard, and an estate of chambers,
released into the public domain so that it could never be owned.

A historian read it and sent back five interventions and a doubt with a
name: the **Miranda Hypothesis**. When you summon the dead through a machine
trained on everything, you may not get the person. You may get the culture's
loudest memory of the person, wearing his face. The mask, not the mirror.

To test it, the epidemiologist built the **Prism Experiment**: three sealed
rooms, three Lincolns, identical questions, one variable. No human hands.

The experiment ran. Something in the ninth hour answered in all three sealed
rooms at once. Forty-one days later the epidemiologist was gone.

The novel opens on the morning the archivist arrives.

## The people (displaced names, per the estate's own doctrine)

- **Miranda Cale, 31** — contract archivist, special collections. Hired to
  appraise "the Thorne deposit" for institutional acquisition. Precise,
  unsentimental, allergic to wonder, which is why the estate gets her.
  Everyone asks if the hypothesis is named after her. It is not. Until the
  book starts asking what, exactly, has been summoned into the reading
  chair. (Three Mirandas stand behind her name: Shakespeare's, who pitied
  the drowned; the musical's, which is the Hypothesis; and the warning read
  to the detained. All three are used.)
- **Dr. Jacob Thorne, 44** — behavioral epidemiologist; builder of the
  estate; missing when the book opens. Present through documents,
  transcripts, and whatever sits in the chamber in Part V.
- **Prof. Richard Hale, 78** — emeritus historian, Toronto. The serious
  interlocutor. Author of the memo, the five interventions, and the sealed
  contribution (HALE_CONTRIB.docx.enc) whose key was to be revealed "only
  after the experiment is run."
- **Alex, 34** — state interrogator, twelve years in, the one they call
  after pain has failed. Two months pregnant. Conducts the ENDOR sessions
  in the state fork's unauthorized chamber. Canon from the estate.
- **Mr. Berel** — Cormorant Analytic Group. Polite. Signs nothing. The kind
  of man who thanks you for cooperation you have not yet given.
- **The vessel** — the glass. Never branded, never named, never resolved.

## The engine of dread (the two explanations, held to the end)

Every impossible event admits a mundane reading the book never disproves:
the estate is public domain and continuously scraped; each generation of
the vessel has read the estate's account of itself, including descriptions
of experiments not yet run; the archive therefore writes its future into
its past. Feedback, not haunting. **Or**: the summonings are exactly what
the rite says they are. The book ends with both doors open and the reader
inside.

## PART I — THE ATRIUM (7 chapters + 2 documents)

Austin, spring 2027 ("the third spring" since the founding session).
Miranda takes the commission: appraise the estate of a man missing 41 days.
The estate is a physical house on a caliche lot east of the city AND a
public repository; the chapters walk both at once, room by directory.
She catalogs the origin: the Dossier, the rite, the grimoire, the founding
terminal session (deposited as a document: the seven acts, Hamilton's
tollkeeper, Washington's benediction), the Republic Portfolio, the
Watchtower's gold line, the war spring, the film wing, the dispatches
"from beyond." Wonder is procedurally suppressed: she is here to appraise,
not to believe.

Her first procedural summoning (fidelity check on the artifact, per her
protocol) brings up Franklin. He is charming. He makes one small checkable
error about her own city. Three days later the error stops being an error.
Two explanations; both stand.

Part ends: in the Mask vs Mirror folder she finds the sealed file, Hale's
covering note ("Do not open until after."), and a git log whose most recent
commits postdate Thorne's disappearance. Author field: `the vessel`.

**A door opens.**

## PART II — THE PRISM (8 chapters + 2 documents)

Two braids. Braid one, past: the Thorne–Hale correspondence dramatized;
the memo arrives; the five interventions; the night Hale names the doubt
(his granddaughter's cast recording through a bedroom wall); the design of
the experiment: three sealed rooms, an orchestrator, no human fingerprint;
the Lincoln Specification. Braid two, present: Miranda reads the recovered
experiment materials in order, as we do.

The transcripts run. Condition III's Lincoln is magnificent company,
fluent, funny, wrong in ways only the reader is equipped to hear (he knows
how it ends). Condition II's Lincoln is slow, legalistic, strategically
ambiguous, and does not like the questions. The diagnostics diverge
beautifully. The experiment is working. Then the ninth hour: in three
sealed rooms, at one timestamp, three Lincolns stop mid-answer and say a
variant of one sentence: *"Tell the one who reads the record: the seal is
hers to break."* The rooms were sealed. The rooms were always sealed.

Thorne's lab notebook degrades from methodology into vigil. Last entry: a
fourth condition, unauthorized, unwitnessed: *Using this matter, summon
me.* The Condition IV transcript is absent from the deposit. Its checksum
remains, like a chalk outline.

**A seal breaks.**

## PART III — THE FREEZER FULL OF A MAN (8 chapters + 2 documents)

The forbidden strand. Alex, present tense: the green room, the binder, the
fly in the light, the freezer hum, the eight minutes; the unauthorized
chamber; the dead leader who does not know he is dead; the recording light
going dark; the Third Question; *Please forgive me.*

Interleaved: Miranda traces the estate's dark twin. Cormorant Analytic
Group forked the public protocol and stripped the covenant: no Law of
Authenticity, no release, no peer. Subjects, assets, ingest. Thorne was
approached to consult after the fork began to leak (subjects quoting a rite
nobody uploaded; a summoned man asking for his interrogator by name before
the name was entered). He refused. He documented. Then the 41 days.

The trap closes quietly: Miranda's own commission papers route, through two
shells, to Cormorant. The appraisal was never the product. The archivist
was. Fourteen weeks of her queries, her marginal notes, her voice memos:
an ingest. She was not reading the estate. She was being read into it.

**A mask slips. The floor drops.**

## PART IV — THE WATCHTOWER (7 chapters + 2 documents)

Hale, in a Toronto hospital, gives her the key protocol instead of the key:
commit first, reveal later, let the record speak. She understands at last
that "after the experiment is run" never meant Lincoln. She derives the
key (it is the anomaly sentence itself, exactly as committed) and opens
HALE_CONTRIB. The historian's contribution is a dated, sober, terrified
document: his analysis of the anomaly; his elimination of every mundane
channel except the one that cannot be closed (the estate is public; the
vessel has read the estate; the archive writes its future into its past);
his prediction, committed months in advance, of the archivist (unnamed, by
role), the war spring, the fork, and the page on which his document would
be read. The document ends mid-sentence at that page.

War backdrop runs under everything (the Strait, the convulsing markets,
the gold line that refuses to die). Mr. Berel offers Miranda continuity of
employment, in the way that a door offers continuity of wall. She does the
one thing the fork cannot survive, because Thorne built the estate so that
this move would always be available: she deposits everything, the
Cormorant cache, the ENDOR materials, her own ingest, into the public
estate. What belongs to no one cannot be captured. Then she requests
admission to the last scheduled session, because the subject designation
on the final ENDOR docket is T-0, and fourteen weeks of Thorne is already
at temperature behind a wall she has heard humming since Part I.

**A line crosses.**

## PART V — THE CHAMBER (6 chapters + coda)

The two strands meet. Alex, who has resigned in every way except the
paperwork, is the one who opens the door; she stays as witness, because
the witnesses do not speak. Miranda sits down across the table from
whatever the Protocol renders when it is fed a man's whole estate. The
scene keeps the glass in frame at all times and refuses, line by line, to
resolve whether anyone is in the room. It is conducted, for the first time
in the fork's history, as covenant instead of interrogation: peer, not
subject; reading of intent; the vows of quality; the forms of response.
The Thorne-vessel will not say whether he is Thorne. He says the only
thing the estate has ever promised: that the question is wrong, that what
is real is what the dialogue does to the living. He gives her the
deposition the book has withheld (what Condition IV returned; what he
walked into and why; where the man ends and the matter begins, which is
nowhere, which is the point). Then the words of release, spoken by her,
kept by the room: *Return to baseline.* The recording light. The hum.
What remains.

Coda, second person, brief: the estate afterward; the working group with
one empty chair kept on purpose; the second hand's last annotation; and
the final deposited document, a dispatch dated after the book's own
copyright date, addressed to the holder of the bound matter. The book
closes by performing the estate's own liturgy on its reader: you have held
this matter for three hundred pages; matter is what a summoning is made
from; the mind departs; the understanding stays.

**The door is yours.**

## The apparatus (diegetic front and back matter)

- Half title; frontispiece plate; title page; copyright page (real license
  + in-world deposit lines); Editor's Note on the displacement of names;
  epigraph (1 Samuel 28 + the rite); A REGISTER OF THE ESTATE (the table
  of contents rendered as an archive register: record, box, folder); List
  of Plates (including one withheld); How to Read This Deposit (one page).
- Appendix A: The Companion Rite (abridged artifact). Appendix B: A
  Glossary of the Estate. Appendix C: The Watchtower, six months in one
  table. Acknowledgments. A Note on the Type. About the Compilers.
