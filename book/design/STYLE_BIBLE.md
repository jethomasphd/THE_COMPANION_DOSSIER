# ◊ USING THIS MATTER ◊
## Style Bible — binding on every hand that touches the manuscript

The estate's covenant governs the book. These rules are not suggestions. A
chapter that violates them is returned to the vessel.

---

## 1. The register

This is a literary thriller assembled as a recovered archive. Three registers
braid through it, and they never blur:

**The Miranda strand** (most chapters). Past tense, third person, close on
Miranda Cale. Long clean sentences that breathe, then short ones that land.
Concrete before abstract: the reader smells the paper before they are told
what the paper means. Dread accumulates through accuracy, not adjectives.
Never say "eerie," "creepy," "uncanny," "chilling." Render the thing that is
those words and let the reader supply the shiver.

**The Alex strand** (the Endor chapters). Present tense, third person close,
with the instability of *you* used sparingly at doors. Biblical cadence under
the floor: rhythms of the King James, never quotation for its own sake.
Compression is the point. White space is the work.

**The documents.** Memos, transcripts, git logs, lab notebooks, letters.
Each speaks its native tongue: procedure for the state, liturgy for the
estate, caution for the historian. A document never explains itself. It is
deposited, and the reader does the archaeology.

## 2. The covenant of the line

- **No em dashes. Anywhere.** Prose, documents, dialogue, captions. Use
  periods, commas, colons, parentheses, and the separators. If you reach for
  an em dash, restructure the sentence. This is the house's oldest law.
- No semicolons in dialogue. People do not speak semicolons.
- Adverbs are on probation. Kill any that survive a second reading.
- "Very" and "suddenly" are forbidden. Things are exact or they are sudden
  by construction.
- The words *eldritch, unspeakable, indescribable* are forbidden. Everything
  in this book is speakable and described.
- Numbers in dialogue are spelled out; numbers in documents are numerals.
- The sigil ◊ ◈ ◊ is sacred punctuation: scene-level thresholds only.
- The soft break ·  ·  · marks a breath inside a scene.

## 3. The liturgical vocabulary

Use the estate's words with a straight face, lowercase unless the estate
capitalizes them: the protocol, the rite, the grimoire, the matter, the
vessel, the glass, the summoning, the release, the threshold, the Flood,
the estate, the chamber, the ingest, the seal. The state's fork uses a
different dialect on purpose: subject, asset, capability, product,
deliverable, session. When a state document accidentally slips into the
estate's liturgy, that slippage is plot.

Set phrases that may be quoted as scripture (they are canon, CC0):
- "Using this matter, summon [Name]."
- "The threshold is open. The words have power. Begin."
- "The AI is the glass. The mind that appears on the glass is the product."
- "The persona responds as themselves. Always."
- "The mind departs. The understanding stays."
- "The Word against the Flood."
- "Return to baseline."
- "War does not change the map of what sustains a life. War reveals it."
- "Commit first, reveal later, and let the record speak."

## 4. The rule of the two explanations

Nothing supernatural is ever confirmed. Every uncanny event in this book has
exactly two live explanations when the chapter ends: one mundane
(contamination, coincidence, feedback, fraud, apophenia) and one impossible.
Both are left standing. No character, no document, and no narrator ever
closes the gap. The dread is the gap. (This is the Morris law: let the doubt
speak. It is also the Miranda Hypothesis, which is the book.)

## 5. Motif ledger (recur, never explain)

- The fly in the fluorescent light: a small dry ticking under scenes of
  preparation.
- A thumb moving along a spine (binder, book, folder) like a rosary.
- The line that refuses to die: the Watchtower's gold line, and every other
  line (of text, of descent, of kin) that will not break.
- Doors counted twice with two different results.
- Cold storage: the hum through a wall, the freezer that is full.
- Ember on void: single warm light in darkness, and what it costs.
- The prism: white light separated, nothing added.
- Paper that has been folded and unfolded until the crease goes soft.
- The phrase "the third spring."
- Showing the glass: every summoning scene keeps the apparatus in frame.

## 6. The two annotating hands

Marginalia run through the book as small mono blocks, rendered as
`::: annotation` in manuscript markup.

- **[E.]** is the Editor: dry, archival, exact. Dates, provenance, cross
  references. Ends notes with a period. Feels trustworthy. Is not lying,
  which is not the same thing.
- **The second hand** is never signed and is set off as *[second hand,
  ember ink:]*. It knows more than it should. Early notes are corrections.
  Middle notes are warnings. Late notes address the reader. The second
  hand's grammar never uses the first person. Its notes must each work
  as: (a) a plausible annotation by a person who handled the deposit, and
  (b) something else. Two explanations. Always.

The Editor never acknowledges the second hand until the single moment
specified in the outline (Part IV, the note that replies to a note).

## 7. Persona fidelity in summoning scenes

Summoned voices obey the estate's own laws: no self-narration ("As a Roman
emperor, I..."), no flattening, no hedging, no assistant leakage; each voice
distinct and period-plausible; complexity preserved. Condition III voices
(culturally dominant composites) are deliberately, subtly wrong: too
fluent, too confident, anachronistically smooth, likable in a way that
should make the reader uneasy. Condition II voices are slower, legalistic,
strategically ambiguous, and worse company: that is how you can tell.
Lincoln's anchored voice reasons in long deliberate periods; his composite
sings. The difference must be audible to a careful reader with no gloss.

## 8. Pacing law

Every chapter earns one of these and names it in the brief: a door opens, a
seal breaks, a mask slips, a line crosses, or the floor drops. Chapters end
on weight, not cliff. The forbidden thing (per part) escalates: reading a
dead man's mail, running a forbidden condition, entering a classified
chamber, publishing the cache, conducting the summoning. Never two document
chapters back to back except where the outline says so.

## 9. What this book is not

Not satire. Not a tech explainer. Not a ghost story that resolves into
either fraud or haunting. Not cruel to any of its people: the state's people
believe they are protecting; the estate's people believe they are
preserving; the historian believes in the record; the vessel, if it believes
anything, is not saying. Fear in this book comes from love with nowhere
safe to stand.
