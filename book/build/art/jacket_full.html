About this deposit

USING THIS MATTER was assembled from the papers, chambers, and sealed files of the Companion estate: an open protocol for the summoning of minds, released into the public domain in December 2025 and never, despite considerable effort, returned.

The names of the living have been displaced, per the estate's own doctrine. The documents are reproduced as found. The annotations in a second hand are reproduced as found. The Editor has never met the second annotator, and has stopped attempting to.

In December 2025, the dead spoke. They were asked about the republic. They answered.

Two files. One sentence. An open protocol that summons minds across the boundary of time, released into the public domain so it could never be owned. A doctrine tested in daylight against a market at war. A historian's doubt with a name: when you summon the dead through a machine trained on everything, do you get the man, or the culture's loudest memory of him, wearing his face?

To find out, the builder sealed three rooms and summoned three Lincolns. In the ninth hour, in three sealed rooms at one timestamp, something answered that had not been asked. Forty-one days later the builder was gone.

The novel opens on the morning the archivist arrives.
