# USING THIS MATTER · A Timeline of the Record

An in-narrative chronology, built so a reader can ground themselves at any point in the book **without being told anything the record has not yet told them.**

**How to use this page.** The timeline is layered. Read the first layer before page one if you like; it contains only what the volume's own front matter and jacket already say. Each later layer is gated: open it only after you finish the Record it is named for. Nothing inside a gate spoils anything beyond that gate.

---

## The estate's calendar (safe from page one)

The estate does not count in calendar years. It counts in springs, from its own threshold: the founding year is the first spring.

| The estate says | The calendar says |
|---|---|
| the first spring | 2025 · the founding year (the estate is founded in its December) |
| the second spring · the second vigil | 2026 |
| the third spring | 2027 · the season of the appraisal, the deposit, and this volume's date line |
| the fourth spring | 2028 |

The count is always from a threshold. When a document in the deposit says "the third spring," it means the spring of 2027.

*A note for the workshop, not the reader: the record preserves more than one edge for the builder's disappearance (a county report's "twenty-fourth," a calendar entry of March 13 "one day before the last day anyone would swear to having seen him," and the executor's March 26). If any of the three is unintentional, Record II box 4 and Record III box 2 are the places to look.*
