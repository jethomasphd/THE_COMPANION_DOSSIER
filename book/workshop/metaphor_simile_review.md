# USING THIS MATTER · Metaphor and Simile Review Table

Workshop material for the human-feel pass. Every figure of speech the sweep surfaced, in book order, each rated for the risk that a reader smells an installed (AI-flavored) flourish rather than a felt one. Line numbers refer to the paragraph sequence of the extracted manuscript text (one paragraph per line, front matter starting at 1); they order the table and locate figures relative to their folder.

**Inventory: 866 figures** (76 high priority, 397 medium, 393 low). 194 route through the body (hands, palm, thumb, breath, mouth, skin).

Rating key:

- **HIGH**: review first. Template constructions repeated to the point of tic, somatic flourishes that feel installed rather than felt, epigrams too symmetrical by half, physically confused images, or clich&eacute;s with a bow on them.
- **MEDIUM**: works in isolation but belongs to an over-used family, or is one notch too polished. Review when the family count (below) says so.
- **LOW**: earned, specific, doing narrative work, or a deliberate motif of the book. Mostly leave alone.

## 1 · Read this first: the pattern-level findings

Individual figures matter less than families. These are the constructions that recur often enough that a reader may start seeing the machine instead of the prose. Raw counts from the manuscript text:

| Construction | Count | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| "the way ..." comparisons | 231 | The book's signature tic. Many are excellent; the density is the problem, and the doubled form (the way X, the way Y) reads as a generative pattern. Recommend cutting or varying a third of them, keeping the trade-specific ones (teller, auditor, ledger) and losing the generic-experience ones (the way you sleep, the way you trust a rail). |
| "as though ..." | 20 | Moderate. Fine at this rate. |
| "as if ..." | 18 | Moderate. Fine at this rate. |
| "like a ..." similes | 65 | Fine in volume; individual offenders flagged below. |
| "which is to say" | 8 | A donnish tic. Twice is a voice; more is a template. |
| "palm flat" gestures | 20 | Deliberate motif (the sounding gesture). Keep, but note it is carried by four different characters, which either means the estate teaches it (good, and R V says so) or the author's hand shows (risk). |
| "thumb ... spine" gestures | 9 | Deliberate motif, explicitly passed hand to hand in R IV/V. Keep. |
| "two explanations" constructions | 6 | The book's epistemology. Keep. |

**The five families to worry about:**

1. **The 'the way X, the way Y' doubled comparison.** e.g. lines 1138, 2156, 989. One doubling per record is a cadence; the current density is a fingerprint. This is the single highest-yield revision target in the book.
2. **The aphorism button.** Sentences engineered to be quoted, placed at paragraph ends: *Measuring was the only prayer he knew* (1140); *Nothing that is only kind is telling you the truth* (954); *a record you cannot verify is a rumor with margins* (1907); *A designation is a curtain...* (1975); *guessing is testimony wearing gloves* (2112). Each is good alone. Ten per record reads as a machine that mints epigrams, which is uncomfortable because the book itself accuses the composite Lincoln of exactly this (line 909). Consider whether that irony is intended and visible; if not, thin them.
3. **Installed somatics.** Body-routed flourishes where the body is decoration, not perception: *faint as a pulse in the jaw* (2040), *the mouth is where the truth lives* (1725), *voice... to the register where the heaviest freight moves* (1732), *She let that cross her face without landing anywhere* (2322). These are the classic AI-flavored moves; each flagged HIGH below.
4. **Abstractions in clothes/with anatomy.** *appetite wearing conscience's clothes* is flagged in-world as emergence and works; but the family (truth walks out wearing procedure, testimony wearing gloves, a wager dressed for church, oligarchy wearing virtue) is large. The clothing conceit is thematically load-bearing (masks!), so keep it, but make each instance count.
5. **The loud-silence paradox.** *the nothing was the loudest note in the folder* (1613) and *the one thing she leaves open is the loudest thing in the room* (1712) are the same figure twice within one record. Cut one.
6. **Verbatim self-repeats that are not motifs.** *the way the tongue returns to the place a tooth was* (391) recurs as *the way the tongue returns to the seat of a lost tooth* (1261). Unlike the palm/thumb/hum liturgy, nothing in the book frames this one as deliberate; it reads as the generator reaching for the same shelf twice. Keep at most one.

**Deliberate motifs: do not sand these.** The hum; the fly in the light; ember on void; palm flat on lid/wall; thumb on spine; crease gone soft; the socket/blank; the chalk-outline checksum; doors and counts; weather-as-fact; the courteous estate; the record personified (it keeps, it does not curate, it waits). The extraction marks these LOW even when they repeat, because the book teaches the reader they are liturgy. The risk is not their existence but any single instance that over-explains itself.
