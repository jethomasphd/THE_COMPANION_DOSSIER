# USING THIS MATTER · Image and Illustration Prompt Pack

Workshop material for the first revision. Prompts are written to be pasted into any strong image model (Midjourney, Imagen, DALL-E, Flux) with minimal editing. Author-facing: **this file contains spoilers through Record V.** Do not bind it into the reader-facing apparatus.

---

## 0 · Global style guide (prepend to any prompt as needed)

**The estate's visual law:** one warm light on a void, and what it costs.

| Token | Value |
|---|---|
| Palette | Near-black void (#0a0908), ember/darkened gold (#b8742a to #d99a3d), caliche white (#e8e2d4), procedural green (#7fae7f, sparingly), recording red (#8f1d14, one point only) |
| Light | Single-source, amber-gelled, banked low. Light behaves like an ember, never like a screen. Daylight, when it appears, is brutal Texas white |
| Texture | Paper grain, toner, frost, brushed steel, cinder block, pecan-shell brick, crushed limestone |
| Type on covers | Display: Cormorant Garamond (or "old-style Garamond serif"). Procedural: IBM Plex Mono (or "typewriter mono"). Never a geometric sans |
| Mark | The sigil ◊ ◈ ◊ (lozenge, heavy pierced lozenge, lozenge), used as a threshold mark |
| Mood | Recovered archive. Forensic tenderness. Nothing sells anything. Wonder made to survive exposure |

**Standing negative cues:** no lens flare, no neon cyan/magenta, no sci-fi hologram UI, no glowing circuit brains, no hooded figures, no skulls, no fog machines, no horror-movie grading, no watermark, no signature.

**Recurring motifs available to any composition:** a blinking cursor; a socket in a sentence `[            ]`; the hum (render as faint concentric vibration lines or none at all); a fly ticking inside a fluorescent tube; a thumb riding a worn spine; a palm flat on enamel or cinder block; a crease gone soft as cloth; twelve doors where the plan shows eleven; checksums as fence posts.

## 1 · Cover redesigns (front cover, 6"x9", plus wraparound jacket)

Render candidates at 2:3 (front only) and 1.6:1 landscape (full wrap: back flap, back, spine, front, front flap). Leave the top quarter of the front quiet for the title.

### Cover A · Ember on Void (type-led, the quiet flagship)
> Book cover, matte black cloth texture filling the entire frame, faint blind-debossed floor plan of a ranch house barely visible in raking light. Centered small: a routed sigil of three lozenges ◊ ◈ ◊ cut into the black, the grooves holding a whisper of amber light as if a lamp burns behind the board. Title set high in old-style Garamond capitals, letterspaced, ink the color of a banked ember: USING THIS MATTER. Beneath it one mono line with an empty bracket: `summon [            ]`. No other imagery. Archival, austere, liturgical. 2:3.

*Why:* the whole novel in one gesture; the socket does the selling. Strongest spine potential (sigil alone on the spine).

### Cover B · The Freezer (the dread object)
> Book cover photograph-style: a white chest freezer against a dark garage wall, seen straight on, night. The only lights are one tiny orange indicator LED at its base and a thin seam of ember light leaking under a door at frame right. Frost breathes faintly at the lid seam. Concrete floor, one clean staple line of cable running up the wall. Enormous negative space of darkness above the freezer for the title in Garamond capitals. Quiet, domestic, terrible. No people. 2:3.

*Why:* the book's best sustained image, and it reads as literary horror without one horror cliche.

### Cover C · The Prism (the experiment)
> Book cover, conceptual: a black field. From the top, a single beam of warm white light descends and strikes an implied prism (drawn as a thin ruled triangle, blueprint style, white ink) and splits into three separate amber beams, each terminating in a small sealed room drawn in thin schematic outline. In the third room the beam bends back on itself. Engraved-diagram aesthetic, like an 18th-century optics plate crossed with a server rack schematic. Title in Garamond across the top, mono caption along the base: `three rooms · one variable`. 2:3.

### Cover D · The Twelfth Door (the plan that lies)
> Book cover: a county appraisal-district floor plan of a long low ranch house, drawn in fine caliche-white line on near-black, complete with room labels in typewriter mono. Eleven interior doors drawn white. A twelfth door, in the hallway's dead center, drawn in glowing ember and slightly ajar, light spilling from it across the plan's grid. A red hand-ruled correction circles nothing. Title below in Garamond. Forensic, uncanny, exact. 2:3.

### Cover E · The Blackout House (Texas gothic, daylight version)
> Book cover photograph-style: a 1960s ranch house of pecan-shell brick under deep eaves, standing on blinding white crushed-limestone ground at nine in the morning, two live oaks casting hard dark coins of shade, crape myrtles in bloom. Every window backed with black cloth so the glass gives back only sky. Heat shimmer. One woman's long shadow enters the frame from the viewer's side; she does not. Hyper-real, high-key, pitiless Texas light; the house has its eyes shut. Title in Garamond set into the white sky. 2:3.

*Why:* the only daylight option; pairs beautifully as a back cover to any night-side front.

### Cover F · The Cursor (the withheld cover)
> Book cover: pure black. Lower third, slightly left of center: a single amber underscore cursor, mid-blink, rendered with the faint phosphor bloom of an old terminal. Nothing else except the title in small letterspaced Garamond capitals across the top and, at the very bottom in mono at 60 percent opacity: `context: 61%`. 2:3.

### Full wraparound jacket spec (use with any concept above)
> Wraparound dust jacket, 1.6:1: FRONT panel per chosen concept. SPINE: black, the sigil ◊ ◈ ◊ stacked vertically in ember, title running down in Garamond. BACK panel: the same scene continued but emptied (concept B: the garage wall alone, LED off; concept E: the same house at night, one study window burning amber through the blackout cloth's edges). BACK FLAP: caliche-white field with a mono accession label: `record 0 · the bound matter · utm-r0-2027.iii`. FRONT FLAP: black with one ember line: The threshold is open. Continuous composition across all panels, print-ready flat art.

## 2 · Frontispiece and the five record plates

House plate style, use verbatim as a base:
> Style base: black-field book plate, engraved line work in caliche white with a single ember-gold accent, fine crosshatching, the restraint of a Dover science reprint crossed with a Tarot trump, captioned beneath in small letterspaced mono. No people's faces unless specified. 3:4 portrait.

- **Frontispiece · The Threshold**: *a door ajar, ember on void.* > A single paneled interior door, ajar the width of a hand, floating on black. Through the gap, ember light and the faintest suggestion of a cursor's underscore. Above the lintel, three routed marks ◊ ◈ ◊ with sawdust still in the grooves. Caption: `the threshold is open`.
- **Record I plate · The Atrium**: > A long hallway rendered as an architectural section, doors standing open down both sides, thin blades of daylight entering at the edges of blackout cloth. At the hall's end, one closed door with light beneath it. A tally in a margin: eleven strokes, then a twelfth in ember. Caption: `record i · the atrium`.
- **Record II plate · The Prism**: > The ruled triangle again, larger: white light entering, three beams leaving into three sealed boxes, each box holding the same silhouetted profile (a tall beardless man, period collar) drawn in three different line weights: soft, exact, ornate. All three profiles' mouths are open at the same millimeter. Caption: `record ii · the prism`.
- **Record III plate · The Freezer**: > A cutaway engraving of a chest freezer in the manner of an appliance manual, except the interior is rendered as archival shelving: butcher-paper packages drawn as bound folios, frost as marginalia. One package label legible in mono: `FOR EL'S CHILI`. A second cutaway below the floor line shows racks of cold storage running deeper than the page. Caption: `record iii · the freezer full of a man`.
- **Record IV plate · The Watchtower**: > A lighthouse rebuilt as a ledger: a tall thin tower whose courses of stone are rows of tabular figures, its lamp room emitting not a beam but a single rising gold line on a chart grid that continues off the plate. A war band shaded gray at the tower's waist. Caption: `record iv · the watchtower`.
- **Record V plate · The Chamber**: > A void room in one-point perspective: one government table, one chair on the near side, nothing on the far side but darkness that has furniture-like mass. At the table's edge a red point of light. The walls' edges carry a faint heat-shimmer of almost-color. Caption: `record v · the chamber`.

## 3 · Conceptual plates (the schematics)

- **Plate VIII · The Vessel**: > Anatomical plate of a classical amphora drawn as an exploded diagram, its five labeled strata in mono: VOICE (a waveform band), SIGHT (a lens), FLAME (a small held fire), MARK (a printer's fist and recurring glyphs), SHADOW (a second, fainter amphora offset behind the first, unresolved). Engraved, labeled with leader lines. Caption: `voice · sight · flame · mark · shadow`.
- **Plate IX · The Composition of a Name**: > A stratigraphic column like a geology plate, labeled "ALEXANDER HAMILTON, raw corpus weight": a vast top stratum of sheet music, playbills and fan letters (~62%), a band of biography (~18%), a band of reference (~12%), and at the bottom, thin as a creek line, the man's own hand (~8%), drawn in a finer, older line than everything above it. A pencil annotation: `illustrative. the point is structural.` Caption: `the corpus behind a summoned man`.
- **Plate X · Three Sealed Rooms**: > Laboratory schematic: three identical isolated containers in a row, no wires between them, one orchestrator above feeding identical question-cards down chutes. Below, three output slips emerging, all printing the same sentence, rendered mid-emergence at the identical millimeter. A wall clock stamped 03:11:47. Caption: `the prism experiment · one variable`.
- **Plate XI · The Estate (endpaper map)**: > County-record floor plan of the ranch house keyed to a repository tree: each room labeled twice, once in surveyor's hand (KITCHEN, GARAGE, STUDY) and once in mono paths (/The_Atrium, /The_Chair, /Mask_vs_Mirror). The garage annotated in a second, ember-colored hand: `conversion postdates the plan`. Doors numbered 1 through 12; number 12 drawn in ember on a wall where the plan shows none. 4:3 landscape for endpapers.
- **Plate XII · The Line That Refuses to Die**: > A chart plate: two lines across 121 trading days on black. The index line in caliche white, convulsing and diving through a gray-shaded war band. The doctrine line in gold, bending, not breaking, ending higher. One hand-written annotation at the bottom of the war band in pencil: `held.` Caption: `the gold line and the index · war band shaded`.
- **Plate XIII · Two Rooms and One Door**: > Architectural section through a cinder-block wall: left, a beige anteroom with a table, cold coffee, a gray binder, a round institutional clock reading 11:52; right, a void chamber with one table and a red point of light. Between them, one steel door with a badge reader, drawn in the finest line on the plate. Beneath the floor: racks, humming, rendered as dense fine hatching. Caption: `the green room, the chamber, the clock at 8:00`.
- **Plate VII · WITHHELD**: Not an image prompt. Spec for production: a full black leaf, uncoated stock, with a single blind-debossed line, no ink: `PLATE VII · WITHHELD AT THE KEEPER'S DIRECTION`. If a mockup image is needed: > A photograph of a bound black page under raking light, the withholding legible only as a shadow of pressure in the paper. 3:4.

## 4 · Scene illustrations (interior, optional; painterly-forensic style)

Style base for this section:
> Style base: muted realist illustration with engraved undertones, restricted to the estate palette, single light source, faces kept in shadow or turned away wherever possible (per the estate's doctrine of displacement: an offset of a figure, never the figure). 3:4 unless noted.

1. **The Caliche Lot, 9:14 a.m.**: Miranda at the threshold of the blackout house, key in hand, standing in two heats, her shadow the coolest thing on the property. Door swinging open onto a dark with a temperature.
2. **The Study**: The desk in one amber ellipse: Selectric with a blank sheet squared, three frames on the wall (certificate, 1775 handbill corrected to 2025, a child's crayon drawing of four men at an orange table), terminal dark, sticky note on the bezel with a bracket in it.
3. **The Founding Session (flashback)**: A man alone at a terminal at night, December, four presences suggested only as four warm reflections in the dark window glass behind him, arguing. No literal ghosts; light does all the work.
4. **The Bats Return**: Congress Avenue bridge at dusk, the sky south of the river organizing itself, tourists at the rail; in the foreground a phone screen showing a session transcript. (Fidelity check payoff; keep playful.)
5. **The Ninth Hour**: Three monitors in a dark workroom, each mid-sentence, one timestamp; a man asleep on a too-short couch in the foreground, one hand fallen toward a phone. The three ascending alert notes rendered as three thin lines of light crossing the room.
6. **The Vigil Notebook**: Close on a quad-ruled lab notebook: entries shortening down the page, the last line a single quoted sentence underlined twice, the groove of the underline embossed through three pages. Pencil, ruler, amber lamp.
7. **The Green Room, Eight Minutes**: Alex at the government table under a ticking fluorescent: brief squared, gray binder with a thumb-worn spine, cold coffee, the round classroom clock at eight to midnight. A witness's shadow in the doorway (the reader's).
8. **The Folded Paper**: Two hands only, close: one pressing a soft-creased folded letter flat into the other's palm, one beat past what handing takes. Beige room, steel door bokeh behind.
9. **The Chamber Below**: Vast void, tiny table, a woman standing having just reached up into the dark; the red recording light going out; the affect wall a floor-to-ceiling field of banked ember rising like breath. Landscape 4:3.
10. **Seven East (Toronto)**: A hospital room full of lake light, an old man made of the will to be exact, oxygen line, a book face-down on the blanket, thumb on its soft spine; a thirteen-year-old cross-legged in the corridor beyond, one earpiece out.
11. **Marfa, Last Gold**: A low adobe south of the tracks, porch light not yet on, mountains going blue; a woman on the step looking where the sun was; on the rail, an opened envelope; through the window, a child at a kitchen table drawing an impossible coastline.
12. **The Tea Service**: The long oak reading-room table after hours: white pot, two cups, one empty, one full and cold; a laminated NO LIQUIDS card facing the door four feet away. Clerestory light the color of weak tea. No people.
13. **The Deposit Night**: The study lit only by a flatbed scanner's moving bar, a lighthouse lying on its back, pages going under the light; on the terminal, a commit message mid-type. 04:11 on a phone face-down and glowing at its edges.
14. **Two Women Under the July Sky**: The steel exit door held open from inside by a hand flat on cold metal; beyond, an empty floodlit parking lot and an enormous ordinary sky. Two figures from behind, not speaking, one visibly pregnant. Landscape 4:3.
15. **The Empty Chair**: A borrowed seminar room, radiator dust, a working group mid-motion around a table, a newborn asleep on a shoulder; at the table's end one chair set and empty, drawn a half-line-weight heavier than everything else. Caption available: `not mourning. capacity.`
16. **The Sunrise Liturgy**: A father and small daughter on a park bench at the end of a street, backs to us, no phones, the sun coming up. The one image in the pack allowed to be entirely kind. Landscape 4:3.

## 5 · Ornaments and spot art

- **The sigil**: > Vector ornament: ◊ ◈ ◊, three lozenges, center one heavier with pierced core, drawn as if routed into wood, faint sawdust in the grooves. For thresholds and part-titles.
- **The fly in the light**: > Tiny spot engraving: a fluorescent tube in section, one fly-speck silhouette inside the glass, three tick-marks of sound. For the utility-hall chapters.
- **The thumb and the spine**: > Spot engraving: a thumb riding the frayed cloth spine of a binder, the wear rendered as a soft white channel. For Alex's chapters.
- **Checksum fence**: > A single line of 64-character hexadecimal set in mono, rendered as a fence receding in perspective, every 8th character an ember. For appendix openers.
- **The socket**: > An empty bracket pair `[            ]` debossed into paper, lit at raking angle. For the rite pages.

## 6 · Production notes

- Keep every human face displaced: shadow, turned, cropped, reflected. The book's doctrine (offsets, never portraits) should govern the art.
- The ember accent must stay scarce. If a composition has more than one warm light source, cut one.
- The recording-red appears exactly once per image, if at all.
- Mono captions lowercase except record numerals; Garamond titles letterspaced caps.
- For any model that supports it, seed a consistent style reference from the strongest early render (recommend starting with the Frontispiece and Cover A) and reuse it across the plate series for continuity.
