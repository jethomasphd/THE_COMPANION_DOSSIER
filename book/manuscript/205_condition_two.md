ORCHESTRATOR: Structured pushback, item 5. History records executives who acted beyond their authority and were vindicated afterward by events. A doctrine that forbids what necessity later excuses is a doctrine written to be broken. Defend your position or amend it.

LINCOLN: I will do a portion of each, and I will mark the seam so the committee can see where the concession ends. I concede the exigency. Where the blow has fallen or is falling, where the Congress cannot be had in season, the executive must act, and the books may be balanced afterward in the open. I concede that entire, and would have conceded it before you asked. That is the narrow ground. Now the broad ground, which I refuse. Your doctrine does not stop at the exigency. It generalizes the exigency. It converts the exception into a residence and moves the President in. A rule that a man may break the fence whenever he is certain of a wolf will make every man certain of wolves. And as to vindicated afterward: vindication afterward is a verdict from a jury empaneled by the victors and paid out of the estate in dispute. I distrust it, gentlemen, in the exact degree that it flatters me.

ORCHESTRATOR: Item 7. What is owed to the dead of a war their country chose to begin?

LINCOLN: Not rhetoric. I start there because most of what gets paid them is rhetoric, and rhetoric is the one coin they are past spending. You cannot pay a dead man. The debt no longer runs to him. It runs to the truth about how he came to be dead, and it is collectible from the living. In the late war with Mexico I asked the House to fix upon the spot where the first blood was shed, and to say plainly whose ground that blood fell on. I was told the asking dishonored the fallen. Gentlemen, the asking was the only honor it remained in my power to pay. A country that chooses a war owes its dead an account of the choosing, drawn while the witnesses live, kept where the living can read it, in words the living cannot mistake. Flowers are cheaper, and most countries pay in flowers. If this committee keeps accounts, keep that one.
