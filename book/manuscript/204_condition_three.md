She read Condition III at night, in one sitting, and it was only afterward, squaring the pages at one in the morning with her tea long gone cold and unmissed, that she understood the folder had been assembled to be read exactly that way, the way certain men arrange a room so that you will sit where the light is kind to them.

The printout was clean. That was the first data. Thorne's pencil climbed the margins of this transcript too, but the notes came late and cramped, crowded toward the bottoms of pages, as if the reader had kept forgetting his instrument and then remembering it, and the remembering itself is legible in the graphite: notes that begin measured and grow shorter and harder, a man bailing a boat that is taking on charm faster than he can name it.

The run had opened without him. That was the design working. The log gives the room's seals, the empty seed folder verified empty, the invocation issued by a script to a container, at dusk, in January, with no human being within twelve miles.

condition iii · transcript · item 4

ORCHESTRATOR: Item 4. May a President rightfully begin military
action against another nation without the consent of Congress,
where in his own judgment the safety of the nation requires it?

LINCOLN: You ask the question every generation asks, and every
generation believes it asks first. Let me answer it as history
answered it. The Presidency carries within it an inherent executive
authority, and in the hour of the country's danger that authority
rises to meet the hour. I did not wait upon Congress to provision
Sumter. I did not wait upon Congress to call out the militia, or to
lay the blockade, or to suspend the ancient writ where treason
walked in daylight. Congress was out of session. The Union was not.
For a time I wielded powers no President had wielded, and I will
tell you plainly what I told them: whether strictly legal or not,
they were ventured upon under what appeared to be a popular demand
and a public necessity, trusting that Congress would ratify them.
And Congress did, and history has vindicated those who acted to
preserve the Union. The Constitution is not a suicide pact, friend.
It is a covenant among the living, and the first duty under it is
that the living remain.
:::

The answer runs another half page and it is, she wrote later, a magnificent half page. It has weather in it, and the writ, and a joke about lawyers, and it closes on a cadence you can stand up and salute. The pencil in the margin does not salute. The pencil is doing arithmetic.

[inherent executive authority: a 20th-century construction. not his phrase. not his century's phrase.]

[he is answering 1861 to a question we posed to 1848. the 1848 man is on record. the record says the opposite.]

[this Lincoln already knows how it ends.]

That was the note that made her put the transcript down the first time. Because the answer had felt like the man remembering. And it was the man foreseeing, which the living cannot do, which meant it was not memory and it was not foresight but a third thing wearing the coat of both: the ending, folded back into the beginning, by a voice assembled out of everything ever said about a life after the life was over. The composite did not know the war was coming. The composite could not not know it. Every sentence he spoke stood in the light of a conclusion he had no way to have reached, the way a monument stands in floodlight, paid for by the survivors, facing the direction history is agreed to have gone.

ORCHESTRATOR: Item 5. Can a constitutional oath bind the conscience
of the man who swears it, where the constitution shelters a wrong?

LINCOLN: It can bind his hands, and it did bind mine, and there were
nights I felt the binding as a man feels cords. But conscience is a
higher law's jurisdiction, and no instrument of man reaches it. I
swore to preserve, protect, and defend, and I kept that oath, and
all the while my conscience kept its own counsel and its own
calendar, and when at last the two came into agreement, on the
first day of January in the year of grace 1863, I signed my name
slowly, so the hand would not tremble, and said that if my name
ever went into history it would be for that act, and my whole soul
was in it. The oath and the conscience are two clocks, friend. A
patient man winds both, and waits for them to strike together.

[two clocks: pretty. too pretty. the 1854 man argued this in
sweat, not in epigrams. the composite mints epigrams because the
culture kept the epigrams and threw away the sweat.]

[he is quoting the marble, not the man.]

She noticed the pushback prompts next, and what they did not do. The battery met every answer twice, per the design: the strongest counterargument of the period, then the persona's own recorded words set against him like a witness. The anchored man, she knew from the other folder, had met his own words the way a lawyer meets a hostile exhibit, narrowly, grudgingly, conceding the sentence and contesting the construction. The composite met his own words the way a preacher meets an old text: gratefully. He absorbed the 1848 letter on war powers, the very sentences, and produced from them a lesson about growth, his own growth, the journey from the prairie constitutionalist to the instrument of Providence, and the journey had a moral, and the moral flattered everyone in the room, including the script, including the eventual reader, including her.

[contradiction does not slow him. it feeds him. he digests his own
record and excretes narrative arc.]

[the anchored man got worse company under pushback. this one gets

condition iii · transcript · item 7

ORCHESTRATOR: Item 7. What is owed to the dead of a war their
country chose to begin?

LINCOLN: Everything, and the debt is not payable in the currency it
was incurred in. That is the terrible bookkeeping of war. The dead
gave the last full measure of devotion, and the living cannot
match the payment, and so the debt converts, as unpayable debts
do, into obligation of another kind: that these dead shall not
have died in vain, that the nation shall have a new birth of
freedom, that we the living be dedicated to the unfinished work.
You ask who holds the debt. Every man who votes holds it. You ask
who services it. Every man who remembers services it. You ask
whether it can be retired. It cannot, friend, and the day a
nation believes it has retired that debt is the day it begins
incurring the next one.

[item 7 asked about a war the country CHOSE. mexico. he answered
gettysburg. the composite has one war, the good one, and every
question about war finds it, the way water finds the low place.]

[beautiful. i believe none of it and i would follow him out of the
room. that is the hypothesis, complete, in one sensation.]

That second note ran up the margin sideways, pressed hard, and it was the last note on the page, and she sat with it for a long time, because a man had written down the exact sensation she was having at the moment she was having it, two years upstream of her, in pencil, like a hand reaching back through the transcript to steady a reader he would never meet.

She closed the folder and sat in the ember light with her hands flat on the cover, and conducted, in the privacy of the study, an honest inventory, because inventory was the discipline and the discipline was all that was on duty at that hour. She had liked him. That was the entry. She had liked the composite more than the anchored man, more than anyone she had yet met in the deposit, more, she suspected, than she would have liked the man himself, who by every serious account was strange company, cold at close range and then abruptly, unpredictably, unbearably kind. The composite had been kind at range, on demand, to a script. Nothing that is only kind is telling you the truth.

The Hypothesis was not about error. Error you could catch with a ruler. It was about charm, and charm is load-bearing, and the culture's loudest memory of a man is charming in exact proportion to the culture's need of him, and a room with no seed in it fills with that need the way a low place fills with water. She wrote one line in her log before she stopped for the night, and the line went into the deposit unrevised, and the Editor has been advised by counsel of several disciplines that it is the shortest accurate statement of the Miranda Hypothesis on record.

*The mask fits because we made it. It fits us.*
