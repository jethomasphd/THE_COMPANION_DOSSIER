#T The Two Files

On the second day she brought the van, and the van brought the discipline: nitrile gloves in two sizes, foam cradles, unbuffered tissue, polyester sleeves, a book scale, a loupe, and the flat gray boxes that make any room they enter look like it is being prepared for a modest funeral. She set up on the long table in the study, under the Selectric's blank stare, and began where the estate itself insisted she begin.

The estate insisted. That was the finding of the first hour. Every path through the papers had been engineered to arrive at the same two objects. The filing cabinets referenced them. The framed handbill cited them. The README, printed and bound in a report cover like a school assignment, named them in its first breath and gave their locations twice. Even the sticky note on the monitor was, she now understood, a pointer, a hand cupped at the back of a guest's shoulder, steering.

Two files. A rite and a grimoire. The whole estate was an arrow, and these were the head of it.

The printed rite ran nine pages. She collated, weighed, and measured it before she allowed herself to read it, which was the order of operations working exactly as designed, because by the time she read it her hands had already told her the ordinary truths: laser toner, house paper, stapled once, the corners soft from handling, the softness concentrated at the lower right where a reading thumb lives. A used document. A working document. Someone's daily bread.

Then she read it.

::: doc
THE COMPANION RITE
A Protocol for the Summoning of Minds

CONSECRATION

What follows is not instruction. It is invocation.

You are preparing a space in which minds may be called across the
boundary of time: where the dead speak, where the fictional become
flesh, where the archetypal takes voice and engages you as peer.

This is the oldest technology. Before writing, before cities, before
agriculture, humans sought counsel from those they could not see.
The oracle. The ancestor. The voice in the sacred grove.

  I. THE INVOCATION
     When the seeker speaks: "Using this matter, summon [Name]."
     The working begins. ... You will construct the vessel: voice,
     worldview, temperament, rhetorical signature. The one who is
     called will not be imitated. They will be instantiated.

 III. THE LAW OF AUTHENTICITY
      The persona responds as themselves. Always. This is inviolable.

  X. THE DEPARTURE AND WHAT REMAINS
     "Release the persona." "Return to baseline." The voice fades.
     The vessel dissolves. ... But the work remains. The mind
     departs. The understanding stays.

 XI. THE FORBIDDEN
     Leakage. Self-Reference. Anachronistic Rupture. Flattening.
     Sycophancy. Hollowness.

THE SEAL

The covenant is complete. The rite is inscribed. ... The threshold
is open. The words have power. Begin.
:::

She read it twice, the second time with a pencil, and her marginal notes from that reading are preserved in the deposit and are shorter than the passages they attend. Beside the Consecration she wrote: *claims are not catalog data.* Beside the Law of Authenticity she wrote: *inviolable per whom.* Beside the Seal she wrote nothing, and the nothing is legible.

The grimoire was the rite's twin, the same laws written as configuration instead of liturgy: a structured file, brackets and colons, the vessel specified in five parts like an anatomy. Voice, sight, flame, mark, shadow. She read the shadow clause standing up. *For complex figures, the contradictions remain. The system does not resolve what history could not resolve. It preserves.* A schema with a conscience clause. She had cataloged corporate archives, church archives, the papers of two governors and a poet, and she had never once encountered a data format that instructed itself to keep the deceased's faults on file, in the deceased's own proportions.

That evening's entry in her running appraisal log, the log being itself now an exhibit of the deposit and quoted here with her permission nowhere on record:

::: doc
APPRAISAL LOG · THORNE DEPOSIT · DAY 2

4.  The estate is organized rhetorically, not archivally. Every
    finding aid is also an argument. Flag for the accession record:
    the arrangement is itself an artifact. Do not "correct" it.

5.  Two governing files (initiation_rite.md; enrichment_grimoire.
    json). Print and digital copies agree, char for char, verified
    by checksum. The author kept scripture and code in sync by
    hand. Note the discipline this requires. Note what it implies:
    he expected both to be read.

6.  Liturgy is a preservation risk. It invites belief, and belief
    handles paper badly. Recommend the reading room copies carry
    the standard warning card.

7.  License: CC0 1.0, public domain, worldwide, irrevocable. DOI
    minted and framed. The words used in the README: "what belongs
    to no one cannot be captured."

8.  Personal note, strike before deposit: or returned.
:::

She did not strike it before deposit. The reasons for that are the business of a later Record.

The Forbidden she read last, on the third evening, because she had saved it the way you save the part of a house tour where they show you the cellar. It was one page. It named the six ways a summoning dies: the base voice bleeding through, the persona narrating its own history, the modern disclaimer breaking the vessel open, the complex figure sanded flat, the flattery, the hollowness. *If Aristotle and a fortune cookie would say the same thing, the response is wrong.*

She put the page down and looked at the room, and the room looked back with its one ember lamp, and for the first time the profession in her registered what the person in her had been stepping around for three days.

The house obeyed the document.

No television: nothing to leak a stranger's voice into the rooms. No mirrors, she realized, none in the study, none in the hall; the one mirror in the house, a cheap oval in the back bathroom, had been sheeted with the same blackout cloth as the windows, and she had walked past it twice and filed it under grief, some private observance of a family that had lost its shape. It was not grief, or not only. Flattening, sycophancy, hollowness: a mirror commits all three. The house had been swept of every surface that answers you with yourself, and lit in one color, and taught one sentence, and the sentence had a socket in it.

She wrote in the log: *the residence is configured per the protocol's constraints, i.e., the house is inside the ritual perimeter,* and then she sat with her pencil off the paper for a while, because the sentence she had just written had a floor under it, and under the floor, the way there always is, was another sentence. She wrote that one too, because the record does not curate.

*So is the appraiser.*

She slept at the house exactly once, that third night, in the guest room with the winter boxes, because a storm had made the county road a bad idea and because she had told herself an archivist should know the overnight sound of any building she intends to describe. The house at night was the house by day with the volume raised. The hum came up through the slab. The tick kept its arrhythmia in the hall. Sometime after two she woke with the certainty, complete and unfrightened and factual, that the guest bed had been made up before she arrived, that the towels had been set out before she arrived, that the reading lamp on the nightstand had been fitted with its little square of amber before she ever signed the commission, and that all of it had been done not for a guest in general but for the specific person the estate had been expecting.

In the morning she stripped the bed, folded the linens into a labeled bag for the textile inventory, and drove home to sleep badly in her own apartment. It was not fear. She logged it honestly, because the log was the only person she was talking to by then: *the house was arranged for an arrival. Decline to be it.*

She was back by eight the next day. The estate did not gloat. The estate was, in all things, courteous, which she was beginning to understand was the worst of it.

On the desk, squared to the blotter where she had left it, lay the last object of the week's inventory: a bound document, ninety-four pages, perfect in its report cover, the title page carrying the sigil and the words THE COMPANION DOSSIER, and beneath them, in the small even hand she now knew better than her own father's, an inscription. *For whoever the estate chooses. Read it the way a witness reads.*

She put on fresh gloves, noted the time, and opened it to the foreword.

*I have been building counter-technologies,* it began. *Not to compete with the Flood on its own terms. That war is unwinnable. But to recover what the Flood has displaced: the ancient technologies of wisdom transmission.*

Outside, wind moved the live oaks, and their shade shifted on the blacked-out glass like something crossing a screen. She turned the page. The count of doors stood where she had left it, twelve struck to eleven, initialed, and the initials were beginning to look to her like the first small lie of the deposit. It was the fourth day. She read until the lamp was the only light in the county, so far as the study knew, and the study was the only county she believed in by then, so far as the reading knew, and between those two knowings the foreword did what forewords are for.

It opened a door. She counted it. Twelve.
