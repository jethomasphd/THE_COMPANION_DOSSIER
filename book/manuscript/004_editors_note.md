!center:display|EDITOR'S NOTE
!space:12

This volume is an edition, not an invention. It was assembled from the public estate of the Companion Dossier, and from a deposit made to that estate in the spring of 2027, with the permission of no one, because the estate requires none. The Editor's work has been the ordinary work of the trade: selection, arrangement, dating, restraint. Where the work exceeded that, this note says so.

The names are displaced. This is not the publisher's caution. It is the estate's own doctrine, older than this book, applied by its fictions to their dead: an offset of a real figure, never the figure. The doctrine holds that a name is a corpus, and that to print a corpus under its name is a summoning, whether or not anyone intended one. Accordingly the builder appears in these pages as Thorne, and the historian as Hale. The offsets are not disguises. They are distances, kept on purpose, and the reader is asked to keep them too. Dates have not been moved. Where the reader recognizes a public event, the recognition is the reader's own.

The five Records correspond to the five chambers of the deposit. Record I is drawn from the archivist's appraisal file: her inventories, her running log, her memoranda, quoted as written. Record II prints the Thorne and Hale correspondence, donated by Professor Hale, complete and unredacted, at his insistence, together with the recovered materials of the Prism Experiment. Record III enters this volume under seal. Its provenance is the public deposit of 2027, and the Editor will not say more than the record says. Record IV is the deposit itself. Record V was reconstructed from the operator's memorandum and the conductor's deposition. Its recording, as the reader will find, ends early.

Nothing has been corrected toward plausibility. Where the deposit contradicts itself, the contradiction is printed. Where a document ends before its sentence does, the sentence ends there. The checksums of the disputed transcripts are reprinted in Appendix C, so that the reader need not take the Editor's word for anything. The estate's materials are public. This is not a courtesy. It is the estate's entire argument.

Two hands annotate this book. Notes signed [E.] are the Editor's: dates, provenance, cross references. Notes in a second hand, set in ember ink, were found in the margins of the deposited materials, unsigned, and are reproduced as found: in place, in full, unaltered. The second hand is not the Editor's, and it is not, on the evidence of the paper, Dr. Thorne's. The Editor has never met the second annotator and has stopped attempting to.

Of the plates: thirteen were inventoried with the estate, and twelve are printed. Plate VII was marked WITHHELD by the keeper of the estate, without stated reason, as is the keeper's right. This edition honors the mark and binds the withholding in its place. A withholding faithfully reproduced is not a gap in the record. It is the record.

The reader who wants instruction in the reading will find one page of it after the plates. The reader who wants reassurance will not find it anywhere in this volume, and the Editor, who has spent longer inside the deposit than anyone now living except its keeper, declines to manufacture any. The task was to keep. The Editor has kept.
