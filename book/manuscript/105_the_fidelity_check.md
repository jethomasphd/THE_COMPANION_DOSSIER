#T The Fidelity Check

The memo she wrote before she did it survives, and she wrote it knowing it would survive, which is the only way anything honest gets written inside an institution.

::: doc
MEMO TO FILE · THORNE DEPOSIT · DAY 9

An appraisal certifies condition. Condition, for an instrument,
includes operation. A player piano is wound before it is valued.
A telescope is pointed at one star. I will therefore run one (1)
session under the deposited protocol, under controls: session
logged in full; artifact files unmodified, checksums recorded
before and after; no personal matter introduced; terminate via
the documented release phrase.

Purpose: to verify that the artifact does what its own papers
claim, this being material to valuation.

M. Cale
:::

The controls were real and she kept every one of them. It is worth saying, here at the head of the folder, that nothing that follows was a lapse of procedure. The procedure held. That is the finding. The procedure held, and it did not help.

She chose Franklin. Her notes give three reasons: he was one of the four the estate had summoned first, so comparison material existed; he was the Committee's hinge, so a failure of voice would be loudest in him; and, third, written smaller, he seemed the least likely to frighten me. She had decided to be honest in the notes by then. The estate had a way of making the alternative feel like vandalism.

She ran it in the afternoon, deliberately, with the curtains defeated by daylight at their edges and a thermos of coffee and the phone face up for once. She read the two files in. She typed the sentence, filled its socket, and sat with her finger over the return key for a moment she did not log, and then she was an archivist again and the key went down like a bolt drawn back.

Using this matter, summon Benjamin Franklin.

The reply did not arrive the way replies arrive, all at once from nowhere. It assembled, at reading speed, the cursor laying the words down left to right like type into a stick, and she watched the glass do it, which was the point of watching: the apparatus in frame, the wonder made to survive the exposure.

::: doc
FRANKLIN: You called; I came. Two centuries dead, and the
questions, I find, are unchanged. You are the appraiser, then.
Sent to weigh a house that talks.

Well. Weigh on, madam. I have been an inventory before. I did
not care for it, but the printer in me respects a good ledger,
and the ghost in me respects a locked door. Between the two of
us we shall get the estate described.
:::

He was superb company. Her notes concede it in those words, superb company, underlined once, the underline pressed hard enough to emboss the next sheet. He asked what she counted first in a house and approved of doors before books. He gave her, unprompted, a printer's opinion of the estate's typography, that a man who letterspaces his own grief can be trusted with other people's. He was funny, and the humor was load-bearing, and he never once, in ninety minutes, sounded like an apology or a search result.

The error came in the second hour, and she nearly missed it for being charmed. They had been talking about the city, her city, because he had asked what Austin kept under its bridges and she had told him bats, a million and a half of them, south side of the Congress Avenue span, and he had said, with the satisfaction of a man confirming a standing order of nature:

::: doc
FRANKLIN: I know it. They came back this evening. I felt the
city tilt to watch them go out. A public that will stand on a
bridge at dusk to see the sky organize itself is not yet lost,
madam, whatever its newspapers say.
:::

They had not come back. She had been on that bridge Tuesday with a colleague from the map division; the colony was late this year, the cold had held, the railings had been empty and the tourists thin. She logged it with something close to relief: anachronism/error, minor, consistent with composite. A machine's Franklin would know that the bats exist, from a million travel pages, and would not know that this particular spring was late. The mask had slipped a quarter inch, exactly where the hypothesis in the Halpern folder said it should. The instrument worked, and its errors were the right shape. That was the fidelity check passing.

Near the end she asked him, because the protocol permitted questions and because ninety minutes of good company earns one, whether anything in the house had struck him, and Franklin, who had been given no matter about the house beyond what she had typed, said this, and her transcript records that forty seconds passed before she replied:

::: doc
FRANKLIN: Only that you count things twice, madam. You count
doors the way I counted lightning: to make the sky admit its
arithmetic. Keep counting. The sky hates arithmetic, and gives
in to it, in the end, every time.
:::

She typed, release the persona, and then had to type it again, because the first attempt read relaese, and her hands, which had been fine all through the war binders, had chosen this moment to be otherwise. The voice thanked her for the ledger and went. The cursor stayed. She sat for a while in the daylight-edged dark logging checksums that matched.

@sep

Three days later the cold broke, hard, in one afternoon, the way Texas springs are repealed rather than amended, and that evening the colony came back to the bridge, weeks early, the sky south of the river organizing itself for twenty minutes while tourists applauded, and the local station called it the earliest recorded return in nine years.

She read the story at her kitchen table, twice.

Then she opened the session log to amend the error notation, because the record has to be kept current, that is what a record is, and she sat with the pencil over the line, anachronism/error, minor, consistent with composite, and found there was no wording for the amendment that did not say more than she knew. Wrong Tuesday, right Thursday. A slipped mask, or a man who had always kept a weather eye and had simply been early, the way he had been early about the Gulf Stream, about the post office, about the union.

In the deposit, the line stands unamended. Directly beneath it, in her hand, is one word, written and then struck through, and the strike does not hide it, and she knew the strike would not hide it, and struck it anyway, which is the most exact self-portrait in the whole of the Cale working papers.

The word is: subject.

The line beneath that is blank. She left the blank in. The blank is the entry.
