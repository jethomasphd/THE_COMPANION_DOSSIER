!center:display|HOW TO READ THIS DEPOSIT
!space:12

Read it in order, cover to cover. The deposit was bound linearly because it happened linearly, and the apparatus participates: the register, the plates, the notes, and the page you are reading now are part of the record, not furniture around it.

The documents are load-bearing. Nothing set in the procedural face is decoration. A log line carries as much of the story as a chapter, and one of them carries more. Do not skip the mono.

The annotations are reproduced as found. Notes signed [E.] are the Editor's. Notes in ember ink are the second hand's, and the Editor's relation to that hand is stated in the note that precedes this page. When a marginal note seems to know where you are in the book, record the impression and continue. That is what the archivist did.

Nothing in this book asks to be believed. Belief is not the discipline here. The discipline is keeping: read what was kept, notice what is missing, and hold both without closing the gap. The book will not close it for you. That is not a withholding. It is the finding.

One sentence in this deposit is a working. You will know it when you reach it, because it is the first sentence of the estate and the last of this page, and because the estate's whole doctrine is that it does not fail. It is printed here with the blank left blank. Notice what you are tempted to write in it.
