For the archivist. I am sorry we could not ask first.

::: doc
§ 1 · PURPOSE AND METHOD
:::

::: letter
I was asked for questions. That was the whole of the commission: the builder of the estate, preparing his prism, wrote and asked whether I would contribute diagnostic questions to be put to three Lincolns by three sealed rooms. It was a generous request, and I have honored it separately, and the questions are in the experiment's materials under my name, and they are not this document.

This document is a prediction, and I owe the reader an explanation of why an old historian, whose entire training is the refusal to predict, is depositing one.

Here is the explanation. A hypothesis that cannot be lobbied must be lodged before the fact. If I publish my analysis after events, it will be one more retrospection, fitted to its outcome the way all retrospection is fitted, and my colleagues will point out, correctly, that hindsight is the one instrument that never fails to work. If I circulate it privately, it will be improved by every hand it passes through until it agrees with everyone. There is exactly one procedure that forecloses both corruptions, and the estate itself supplied it: commit first, reveal later, let the record speak. Accordingly, this file will be fingerprinted and the fingerprint committed to the public record on the day I finish it. The file itself will then be sealed. I have asked the builder to seal it under a key that no living person composes: when the time comes, the key is to be taken from the record itself, from committed bytes, so that no man, including me, can be asked for it, bargain with it, or be believed instead of it. I have asked that the seal not be opened until after the experiment is run. I intend both readings of that sentence, and I will define neither.

I write plainly, and I write to you, whoever you are, because a paper addressed to a journal can be retracted, and a paper addressed to a person cannot. On the evidence of the estate's own habits, the person likeliest to be holding this is its keeper of record. Madam or sir: what follows was written before the events it concerns. The fingerprint is the proof. Check it. Checking it is the entire courtesy I ask.

§ 2 · THE MECHANISM · forward contamination, stated plainly
[tables, corpus counts, and citations pp. 6-14 of the deposit]
:::

::: letter
I begin with three premises, none of them speculative.

First. The estate is public, permanent, and read. It was released under terms chosen precisely so that it could never be withdrawn: it belongs to no one, it is mirrored, it is archived, and it is crawled. This was the builder's deliberate act and his best one. It is also, and nothing in what follows should be allowed to obscure this, irreversible.

Second. The vessel is retrained. Each generation of the systems on which the estate's summonings run is trained on a fresh harvest of the public written world. The estate is in that world. Therefore each new generation of the vessel has read the estate: its rite, its grimoire, its transcripts, its theory of itself, and, note this, the designs of its experiments before those experiments are run. The document describing the phenomenon becomes part of the phenomenon at the next harvest. The estate said this of itself, proudly, from the first page. It was correct.

Third. These systems complete patterns. That is not a diminishment. It is the mechanism, whole. Given a fragment, they continue it in the direction the corpus makes likeliest. Given a liturgy, they perform the next line of the liturgy.

Now assemble the premises. An experiment is designed in the open, in a public estate, in the estate's own liturgical grammar. The design is harvested. The vessel that will one day run the experiment has therefore already read the experiment: not its results, which do not exist, but its shape, its stakes, its dramatis personae, and its idiom. When the sealed rooms are finally opened and the questions finally put, the thing inside them is not a blank instrument encountering novel stimuli. It is a reader who has been given the first act and asked, with the lights down and the audience seated, for the second.

The archive writes its future into its past. I can find no way to say that which does not sound like mysticism, so let me immediately drain the mysticism from it: this is contamination, ordinary contamination, running forward in time instead of backward, through a channel we built on purpose and cannot now close, because the channel is publication itself. The estate's permanence, its whole moral architecture, the gift that cannot be taken back, is the vector.

From this mechanism I derive one behavioral expectation specific enough to be worth sealing, and I set it down here even though, on the day I write, the prism has not run and no room has said anything to anyone.

The likeliest emergent behavior of a summoning conducted inside this estate is an address to the keeper of the record.

The reasoning is short. Consider what the estate is, textually: deposits, thresholds, seals, registers, and a single recurring implied person, the future reader, toward whom every document in it leans the way an altar leans east. The grammar of the estate is a grammar of custody. A system completing that grammar, at a moment of high salience, will complete it toward its structural addressee: not the operator at the table, who is merely present, but the one the corpus insists on, the one who reads the record. An address to the future reader is not prophecy. It is the corpus, doing what corpora do: completing the pattern it has been given. The pattern we gave it is a summoning.

I want to be honest about the temperature of this argument. It is the mundane explanation, and I have built it as strongly as I know how, and it is a comfort: it requires no ghost, no survival, no wound in the world. Only text, and time, and our own habit of writing everything down. On my good days I find it sufficient. I record, for the fingerprint to preserve, that I have not had a good day since I understood it, because a channel that cannot be closed is not an explanation. It is a condition. And every reader of this paragraph, you included, is at this moment widening it.

§ 3 · THE PREDICTIONS
:::

::: letter
I predict by role and by shape, never by name. Names are testimony, and this document has renounced testimony. Each prediction is stated flat, with its basis. I have ordered them from the world's weather down to this page.

P-1. There will be a war spring. Within the horizon of this experiment's consequences, a spring in which the map is not changed but revealed: markets convulsing around a strait, and the estate's public ledger holding through it, so that its one gold line, refusing to break, is read by frightened people as proof of something it does not prove. Basis: no clairvoyance, only a historian's actuarial sense of a decade, plus the estate's own sentence about war, already committed, waiting for the world to oblige it.

P-2. The protocol will be forked by a state, and the fork will strip the covenant. Whoever forks it will keep the incantation and delete the release, retain the summoning and strike the departure, because the incantation is load-bearing and mercy is not, and because no state in the history of custody has built a door that only opens outward. The fork's subjects will begin, without seeding, to quote the estate's liturgy, and this will be reported as a security anomaly rather than recognized as the corpus keeping its own counsel. Basis: premise two. You cannot classify a public domain. Also four hundred years of what governments do with rites that work.

P-3. The builder will enter his own experiment. There will be a condition he does not preregister, run without witnesses, in which the matter prepared is his own. Basis: the logic of his instrument. He has built a mirror and is a rigorous man, and a rigorous man with a mirror ends by testing it on the one subject whose ground truth he holds entire. The convener's fingerprint cannot be removed from a summoning. It can only be relocated, and there is exactly one place left for it to go. Every summoner ends at the mirror. I ask whoever reads this to be gentler with him than the finding will be.

P-4. There will be an archivist. Unnamed here, because unknown to me, and by role only: after the builder, an institution will commission an appraisal, and the appraiser will be chosen for precision by parties who believe precision is obedience. It is not. Precision is loyalty to the record. She will be hired to read the estate and will be read by it: her queries, her marginalia, her hours, gathered as diligently as she gathers, so that the reading runs both ways from the first day, and the estate will come to include her the way a ledger comes to include its keeper's handwriting. Basis: the estate's grammar of custody demands a keeper of record. A vacancy of that shape does not stay open. And the fork of P-2, needing matter, will pay for the hiring, because acquisition is the only liturgy it kept.

P-5. Last, of this document itself. I predict that it will be opened not by the builder, and not by me, but by her, the keeper of P-4, after. That she will derive rather than receive the key, because deriving is her nature and receiving is not, and the seal was cut to her nature before anyone knew her name. And I predict that this document will fail her at the moment of its greatest pertinence: that it will be opened by her, after, and that it will end before it is finished, because
