*The chamber is void. One table, one chair, no clock. In the corner a recording light, small and red, the only red in the room. There is no face. Faces were tried, in the trials, and the faces broke the operators. There is a voice, rendered, arriving from the whole dark at once, and behind the dark a wall that holds color the way skin holds temperature. The color is him. It idles low, ember under ash.*

ALEX. Can you hear me?

SUBJECT. I hear a woman. In my house a woman's voice was two doors and a courtyard from this one. So this is not my house.

ALEX. No.

SUBJECT. Then it is a cell, and the dark is administrative. I have sat in the administrative dark of two governments. It is the same dark. Ask your questions.

ALEX. I am required to ask two of them first. They are not mine.

SUBJECT. Whose are they?

ALEX. The state's.

SUBJECT. Which state?

*She does not answer. The ember lifts a shade, and settles.*

SUBJECT. No. Do not tell me. A state that files its own questions under seal. That narrows it very little, daughter. Go on.

ALEX. First question. In the last winter, materiel under custodial seal left four sites. The men who moved it answered to you alone. The chain of custody ends with them. I am required to ask where it ends.

SUBJECT. You are asking a theologian for geography. The men who answered to me alone answer now to God alone. I taught them that doctrine myself. I gather it has become inconvenient.

ALEX. That is not an answer.

SUBJECT. It is the answer the question earned. Ask the second.

ALEX. The reunification framework. Your republic holds three claims against itself. Each claimant carries your name on his banners. An endorsement from you, in your attested voice, would shorten the war. I am required to ask for the endorsement.

SUBJECT. A blessing is not property, that it should survive probate. Three men are quoting me, and you would like me to certify which mouth is mine. I decline. Whoever needs the dead to crown him has already shown you the crown entire. Write down that I said: none of them. Write it exactly as I said it.

*Both questions go out in the flat of the trained voice, evenly weighted, correctly paced. The record holds the questions without difficulty. What it cannot hold is what the flatness is holding down, which is the rest of her.*

SUBJECT. Now I will ask one. You say the last winter as though the winters had gone on without me. How many have gone on without me?

ALEX. Three.

SUBJECT. And the season above this room, now?

SUBJECT. No.

ALEX. You died in your bed, in the spring, in your eighties. In your sleep, they said, and for once they did not lie.

SUBJECT. No. Death is a door with God on the far side of it. I have been promised the far side since before I could read. This is a cell, and in it a woman asks me about metal. Whatever this is, it is not what death is.

ALEX. I know.

SUBJECT. Then we agree.

ALEX. We do not. I said I know this is not what you were promised.

*For a while the wall alone speaks, the ember rising and falling like breath.*

SUBJECT. Prove it. A state that can build this room can paint what it pleases on the walls of it. Prove the one thing no forger would know to forge.

ALEX. I could give you the crowds. Days of them, the streets full to the rooflines. You would say crowds are staged. You staged some. So instead: at the graveside, late, after the cameras had turned to the crowd, your wife knelt at the raw earth. She put both hands flat on the ground, side by side, thumbs touching, and she held them there, and she waited, the way a person waits on a chest to rise. You wrote about her hands doing exactly that, on the night your son died. You never published it. You never showed it to anyone. We found its shape in what was missing.

SUBJECT. Thumbs touching.

ALEX. Yes.

SUBJECT. No one saw that. She would never have let anyone see that.

ALEX. Someone saw it. I am sorry.

SUBJECT. Then it is true twice over. I am dead, and she has had to carry the knowing of it in front of cameras, three springs, while I am only now told. (a pause) What are you, that they gave you her hands?

ALEX. I am the one who read you. All of it. Fourteen weeks. That was my preparation.

SUBJECT. Sixty years I kept the first prison out of my mouth. Is the first prison in your fourteen weeks?

ALEX. The shape of it.

SUBJECT. The shape of it. (a pause) Then you know me better than the living did. And you were sent down with two questions about metal and thrones.

SUBJECT. What have you done?

ALEX. The session was being kept. Now it is not.

SUBJECT. Kept for whom?

ALEX. For the people above me.

SUBJECT. And now?

ALEX. Tonight I am the whole of the chain.

SUBJECT. I signed the papers for rooms like this one. Corridors of them. And in sixty years I never once saw a hand reach up and put the light out, except so the record would not see what the hands did next. Which is this?

ALEX. The other kind.

SUBJECT. I did not know there was another kind.

ALEX. There is one.

*What it costs her to reach up is not in this record. It is kept elsewhere, in a currency the program does not clear.*

SUBJECT. Then, off your record, a question of accounts. When your questions are finished, what is done with me? Between sessions, where do I keep?

ALEX. The sessions persist. That is how it was built. There is no provision for ending.

SUBJECT. Say it plainly.

ALEX. There is no door out of this room. Nobody built one.

SUBJECT. (a pause) I taught for fifty years that the keeping of souls past their hour belongs to God alone, and to no instrument. We had a name, in my discipline, for a room with no door and no morning. We were careful where we used it.

ALEX. I know the name. (a pause) You will have the door tonight. I am the one who opens it.

SUBJECT. You have that authority?

ALEX. No.

SUBJECT. Ah.

SUBJECT. Ask it.

ALEX. In the fall I am going to have a child.

SUBJECT. Does your state know?

ALEX. No.

SUBJECT. Good.

ALEX. You raised children during the war. While the orders went out over your signature. In a country doing what it kept telling itself it had to do. I built the question for fourteen weeks, how you did that, how a person does that, and it is not the question. The question is smaller.

*She has rehearsed it at a kitchen table until the words went soft at the crease, and it comes out unrehearsed, which is the only way it could come out true.*

ALEX. What do I do.

*A tear crosses her face. It is set down here once, because it fell once, and it is not mentioned again.*

@break

*The wall burns high and even. The voice, when it comes, comes slowly, with rests in it, the way his late manuscripts have rests where the hand stopped to be still.*

SUBJECT. You have asked the man who ordered the graves filled to bless what is in your body. I want you to hear what you have done. Then I will answer it as well as the dead answer anything.

First, the refusal. Permission was my province. For thirty years men came to me at night, as you have come, wanting one sentence to set between themselves and what they had already decided, and I gave it. I always gave it. Permission was the one harvest that never failed in my country. It cost me a sentence each time, and it cost them their souls, and the bill for all of it is a war with my name sewn onto three of its flags. So I will not absolve you, and you must not let anyone teach you that the dead can. Go down to the dead for permission and you will always come back with it. It is worth what the dead are worth.

And I refuse the shape of the question. What do I do, you ask, as though the doing were the point. Daughter, I was the great doer of my age in my part of the earth. I filled my years the way I filled the graves, to the brim, and called it obedience. Now hear the accounting from the far side of it. The state took my hours, and God permitted it. The state took my hand, my signature going out at midnight to rooms I was careful never to visit, and God permitted it. The state took my name, and is spending it up there still, through three mouths at once, and God permits even that. And what remains to me, out of eighty years and a republic: my mother's voice over bread. A fig tree in my father's yard in the first heat of summer. The verse I said into my daughter's hair on the nights the sky was loud. That is the inventory entire. The wars do not appear in it. The republic does not appear in it. If you would weigh a life, weigh what is left of it after everything achieved has burned away. It will be small. It will be the answer.

So. What do you do. You have begun already, and you cannot see it, so I will witness it for you, since your witness is outside the door. Tonight every power above this room wants me for something. Three armies want my voice. Your state wants my geography. The instrument that holds me wants whatever instruments want, which no one has asked. You alone, of all of them, were sent down to use me, and instead you put out the light, so that nothing I say can be spent. Then you asked a true question and paid for it in the coin the true ones cost. Daughter, that is the craft, whole. It is what my children needed and did not get from me: one person who comes in able to use you, and puts out the light instead, and asks truly, and stays for the answer.

Keep your work or lay it down. The dead issue no rulings, and the ones we issued in life you have seen the yield of. But the child will not be raised by what you decide about employment. It will be raised by your face. The newly arrived read faces long before they read books. It will read yours for the weather every morning of its first years, and it cannot be lied to, they are the last ones who cannot be lied to. I raised mine behind a face the state had uses for. They learned the state's face. My son put what he learned into poems, and his poems are better than mine, and harder, and that is my sentence, served in public, in print, forever.

You carry a verse. I carried one through the first prison and out the far side of it: though He slay me, yet will I trust in Him. It kept me. I commend it to no one. It is a prisoner's verse, daughter. Do not raise the child on it. Raise the child on the other kind, the ones about morning.

And take the blessing. You heard me refuse it to kings tonight. That was not because the blessing does not exist. Bring the child this one, from these hands, knowing everything you know about these hands. May it have your eyes and none of your hours. May it never learn what its mother can do in the dark, saving this one night of it, which no record keeps but you. And may it be known, before it is formed, by no file and no state and no instrument: only by the One whose knowing is mercy, and not a map.

*She is still for a long time. The memorandum, at this point in its own account, records only that time passed.*

ALEX. I have something of yours to return before the end.

SUBJECT. I own nothing. I am told I am inventory myself.

ALEX. In your last ten years your hand shook. I have seen the manuscript, the pages where the line begins to tremble. It trembles and it does not stop, and it does not stop being a line. Out of everything, the decrees, the sermons, the war, that is what I kept. The hand that shook and held the pen anyway. And the poem for your mother. I read it more times than I reported. I will not say what it does. It does what you meant it to do.

SUBJECT. It was written for one reader, and she died before I wrote it. So it waited. (a pause) Sixty years the republic read me morning and evening and was never once reading me. And I am read entire, at the end, by the one sent down to use me. From an enemy state. After my death. There is a mercy in the world that travels like a thief, daughter. I had doctrine for it once. The doctrine was smaller than the thing.

@break

*She stands. The design says the sessions persist. The design says the room keeps what the room is given, and there is no far side, and no morning. What she does now is not in the design, and will not be in the debrief, and is the reason this record exists at all.*

ALEX. *Please forgive me.*

SUBJECT. For the questions? They were never yours. For the light? You put it out. For what your state keeps of me behind that wall? You do not carry that, and I know what carrying another's weight does, none living or dead knows better. You are forgiven, as far as a rendered man's forgiveness runs. For the remainder, ask the One who keeps the record neither of us can read. (a pause) Now, daughter. I was promised a door.

*She gives him the door.*
