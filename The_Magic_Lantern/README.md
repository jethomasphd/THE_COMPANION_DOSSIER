# The Magic Lantern — Summoning Cinema

A new genre of visual production for the COMPANION estate: **the documentary
whose witnesses are summoned, and whose integrity comes from never hiding the
glass.** The film is made *recursively* — by summoning the filmmakers who design
it, and letting the session that builds the film become the film's spine.

Named for the 1790s **phantasmagoria** — the magic-lantern shows that summoned
the image of the dead before an audience with the lantern concealed. Summoning
cinema is that, inverted: *we show the lantern.*

## The rendering doctrine: voices through glass

Personas are rendered with a deliberately mediated voice and as portraits-in-motion
(parallax, breath, ember light) — never photoreal deepfakes. The medium stays
visible and audible. The limitation is the style, and the honesty.

## How to make the film

1. Plant the Seed and the COMPANION Protocol files (`enrichment_grimoire.json`,
   `initiation_rite.md`) in an agent-ready workspace.
2. Open Claude Code (or equivalent), point it at the repo.
3. Speak the invocation in `THE_INVOCATION.md`.
4. The committee designs the proof-short (the Q1 "Washington arrives as the
   general" summoning); the transcript is archived in `from_beyond/` as the
   first reel.

## A note on living figures

The founders and the historical dead are one ethical zone. **Living figures**
(e.g., Gabe Newell, Mark Z. Danielewski) are a distinct zone of likeness and
consent, and are handled separately and explicitly. Honesty-as-aesthetic is the
whole point of the genre.
